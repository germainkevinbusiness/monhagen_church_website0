Church founder Paul D. Laplante & Prisca Laplante From Haiti to Middletown in Ministry.



Leading Our Flock with Faith and Education. Bishop Paul D. Laplante is a dedicated leader within the Middletown, New York community.
His passion for music and ministry is evident in his role as Pastor of the FHCOGOM since 2004.
Not only does Bishop Laplante guide his congregation, but he's also actively involved in broader Christian outreach.
In 2019, he co-founded and currently chairs the American League of Evangelical Pastors (ALEP).
Throughout his career, Bishop Laplante has continuously expanded his knowledge. He began with an Associate Degree in Ministry from World Evangelism Fellowship in 1997. He then went on to earn a Bachelor's degree in Theology and Bible knowledge from Dr. Edward DeVries' School in 2008.
In 2009, he became an ordained Pastor with the Church of God.
His dedication to learning continued with a Doctorate in Philosophy from Saint Luke Evangelical School of Biblical Studies in 2014.
Bishop Laplante's impact extends beyond the church walls.
He actively serves on the Clergy Department Task Force at Garnet Health Medical Center (formerly Orange Regional Medical Center).
He's also a duly accredited Clergyman recognized by the New York City Clerk's Office. Education is another cornerstone of Bishop Laplante's life.
He holds an Associate's degree in Liberal Arts from SUNY Rockland Community College, followed by a Bachelor's of Science in Communications from SUNY Empire State College in 2013.
He furthered his academic journey with a Master's of Arts and an Advanced Certificate in Work and Public Policy from SUNY Empire State College in 2016.
Bishop Laplante and his wife Prisca warmly welcome you to the church website. They encourage you to explore and feel free to contact them if you have any questions. Bishop Paul D. Laplante & Prisca D. Laplante
