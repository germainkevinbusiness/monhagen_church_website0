New to faith in God? Understand the core beliefs



The Bible: Begin familiarizing yourself with the Bible. It's the central text of Christianity. Starting in the New Testament with the Gospels (Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John), they will provide you with a strong foundation for understanding Jesus's life and teachings. Understanding that the bible is composed of the old testament, and the new testament.


The Nature of God: Explore the concept of the Trinity: God the Father, God the Son (Jesus Christ), and God the Holy Spirit. Grasp the idea of God's love, grace, and forgiveness.


Jesus Christ: Learn about Jesus's life, death, and resurrection. Understand his role as the Son of God and the Savior.


Salvation: Discover the concept of salvation through faith in Jesus Christ. Understand the idea of forgiveness of sins and eternal life.


Essential practices of a christian believer


Prayer: Learn how to communicate with God through prayer. Understand different types of prayer, such as praise, thanksgiving, confession, and supplication.


Bible study: Develop a habit of reading and studying the Bible. Consider using study guides or joining a Bible study group.


Fellowship: Connect with other Christians through church attendance and small groups. Build relationships with fellow believers for support and encouragement.


Church community: Finding a good church community is very important. This helps with fellowship, learning, and support.


Key Concepts to help better understand the christian faith


The concepts are:
Grace: Understanding that God's love and forgiveness are a gift.
Faith: Trusting in God and his promises.
Love: Learning to love God and others.
Forgiveness: Understanding the importance of forgiving others, and receiving Gods forgiveness.


Where to start my journey as a new believer


The Bible:
The Gospel of John: This book provides a beautiful and profound introduction to Jesus Christ.
1 John: This book emphasizes God's love and how to live as a Christian.
Psalms: This book offers prayers and praises that can connect you with God.


Important considerations:
Be patient with yourself. Growing in faith is a lifelong journey.
Ask questions.
Don't be afraid to seek answers from pastors, church leaders, or experienced Christians.
Seek out resources.
There are many books, websites, and apps that can help you learn more about Christianity.
